ABOUT

I'm Juan J. Ramirez, a UX Designer and Product Professional with a proven track record of designing and shipping high-value software solutions.

I have 6 years of experience helping different companies to define and create their products.
I have worked in several tech verticals including Enterprise Software / SaaS (Procore), InsureTech (Coverwallet & Coverpocket), E-Commerce (Seconds.com), Edutainment (Carnegie Mellon), Unified Communications (Contactive), Market Research Tech (Usuallee) and Digital Media (ELTIEMPO.COM).

I hold a Master's Degree in Entertainment Technology (Design + Human Computer Interaction) from Carnegie Mellon University and a Bachelor's in Business Administration from CESA Business School.

SAZHIMI

Sazhimi is a personal project that I started on December of 2014. Sazhimi helps you to bulk export Shazam Tags into Spotify Playlists. This feature is just partially implemented in Shazam (single exports, playback only within the app), likely beause of the license restrictions. While doing Sazhimi I explored and experimented with different methods for procedural flows, and created my own flow method called "restrictive progress" in which users are restricted from going back in the flow and repeat an action.

Roles: Product Designer, Full-Stack Developer
Technologies: Python / Flask Framework, HTML, CSS, jQuery

THE LAST EGG

The Last Egg is a digital playground game for two teams of 3 players that uses the PlayStation Move Motion Controllers. The game consists of 3 rounds of 1 minute each.Both teams have to compete to get the last egg : a randomly chosen PS Move with the white light. We created the game as a project in Carnegie Mellon University and ket working on it. The game was chosen as a finalist of the IndieCade Game Festival 2014.

Roles: Game Designer, Sound Designer
Technologies: Adobe Audition, Unity, Paper Prototyping.

REMIXER

Remixer is a a Human Computer Interaction project in which we explored the different uses of prototying techniques + novel interaction methodologies to come up with a sample product that helps users to mix cocktails. Insta-Back

In the midst of the controversy that surrounded Instagram logo change, I was able to design a mechanic to recover the old logo. To achieve this I used the built-in iOS Safari Bookmarks and a deeplinking redirection that worked based on the user agent. This "tiny-hack" got super viral and was featured in hundreds of publications. his end-point receives thousands of daily hits, which demonstrates the success of this small project.

Roles: Front-end Developer
Technologies: HTML, CSS, jQuery / JavaScript
